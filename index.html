Radiohead is an English rock band formed in Abingdon-on-Thames in 1985. The band consists of Thom Yorke (vocals, guitar, piano), Jonny Greenwood (lead guitar, keyboard, other instruments), Colin Greenwood (bass), Ed O'Brien (guitar, backing vocals), and Philip Selway (drums, percussion). They have released nine studio albums, sold more than 30 million albums worldwide, and won three Grammy Awards from 20 nominations. Radiohead have been described by The New York Times as "the most important band of the 21st century".
